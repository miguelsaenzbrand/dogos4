WELCOME TO DUBLIN DOGOS RF


We dream of a world without walls. We believe that no one should be an outsider. And that’s why Dublin Dogos was born, because we want to be the change we wish to see in the world translated into the sport we love the most.

Our aim is to be the next big thing in Irish rugby. A team formed by expats and Irish players, playing together behind one jersey, the fierce Dogo!

Dogos is not an ordinary club but a link between the Irish and the expats communities, serving as a meeting point for migrants and Irish people who love the sport of rugby, fostering integration and promoting social inclusion through this discipline.

One Club, All Countries!



The team functions as a “permanent Barbarian” team, combining talent from different nationalities to compete at the highest level and bring together the expats and rugby communities in Ireland.

2017 was the year of the Dogo. It saw the birth of the project alogn with the following milestones:

  • Deal with Coolmine RFC. Dogos will train and play home matches at Coolmine RFC in Ashtown, one of the finest rugby pitches in Ireland.

  • Affiliation with DCU Force. Dogos will be the team for DCU Force, including current and former students at a club level. DCU Force will give the Dogos the depth and strength necessary to compete at the highest level.

  • Dogos Opening ceremony. In the form of a Tag Rugby tournament, with +400 attendees and a massive Argentinian ‘asado’ and South African ‘braai’ (BBQ). Click here for pictures!

  • Leinster league registration. Dogos will compete with two teams in the Leinster Metropolitan League. The first team will compete in the Metro 3 Division and the second team will compete in Metro 11. Join us!

THE 3RD ‘HALF’


We are introducing a new concept into Irish rugby: the 3rd ‘halves’. This is a social gathering practiced in Argentina after every match, where the home team invites the opposition team to enjoy a post-match meal, furthering camaraderie among clubs irrespective of the result. Dogos will organise 3rd halves after every home match, offering the opposition team a complementary meal at Coolmine RFC. Dogos 3rd halves will include Argentine ‘asado’, a slow-cooked barbecue which will captivate players and attendees, expanding the Dogo concept and associated brands. This unprecedented practice Irish rugby will elicit media attention and increase crowd attendance into the Dogos’ matches.
